Privacy Policy — Last updated April 25, 2026

Overview

Wordly is a word-of-the-day app. We take your privacy seriously. This policy explains what data the app accesses and how it is used.

Data We Collect

We do not collect any personal data. Wordly does not track you, does not use analytics services, and does not send any personal information to our servers.

iCloud & CloudKit

Wordly uses Apple CloudKit to deliver the daily word content (definitions, pronunciations, etymologies). This content is read-only and public — it requires no sign-in.

Your game progress (streaks) and bookmarks are stored in your own private iCloud database via CloudKit. This data belongs to you, lives in your iCloud account, and is never accessible to us.

Local Storage

The app stores your UI preferences, chosen language, and notification settings in UserDefaults on your device. This data never leaves your device except as part of your standard encrypted device backups.

Recently viewed words are cached locally on your device using SwiftData to enable offline access. This cache is never transmitted anywhere.

Notifications

If you enable daily reminders, the app schedules local notifications using Apple's notification framework. No notification data is sent to external servers.

In-App Purchases

Purchase transactions are handled entirely by Apple's StoreKit framework. We do not receive or store your payment information.

Third-Party Services

Wordly does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.

Children's Privacy

Wordly does not knowingly collect data from children under 13. The app contains no user-generated content or social features.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a revised date.
